Nigerian Buns

Nigerian buns are a type of sweet, fried snack that is popular in West Africa. The batter is made from flour, baking powder, milk, and sugar, while the flavor can be nutmeg, cinnamon, vanilla, or any other flavoring of choice.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose Flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoons salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil enough for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Mix all the dry ingredients together in a bowl – flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add the wet ingredients – eggs, melted butter, and milk. Mix together until combined.
  3. Heat enough oil in a large but fairly deep skillet over medium heat until the oil is about 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  4. Gently scoop little balls of batter inside the oil. When they are brown on one side, flip them and allow them to continue to cook until they are browned on both sides. I use an Ice cream scoop.
  5. Once done, remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel or a cooling rack to drain off any excess oil. Enjoy!

For Best Results

Tips for Buns:
  1. Don’t make big balls else the buns will not cook thoroughly on the inside.
  2. The oil that is too cool will soak up way too much oil and oil that is too hot will just crisp your buns too quickly without cooking it through on the inside. So if you are in doubt about the temperature of the oil, it’s okay to test the temperature of the oil with a quick read thermometer to ensure it isn’t too cool or too hot.
  3. If you don’t have a cooking thermometer, you can test fry one scoop of the batter before adding the remaining. That will help determine if your oil is the perfect temperature for frying.
  4. These are best eaten the day you make them. However, you can keep them for up to 1-2 days. They will still be okay but not as fresh as the first day you make them.
Tips for Chin Chin:
  1. Don’t be in a haste to eat your chin chin, let it cool down properly in order for you to enjoy the crunch.
  2. If you are using powdered milk just dissolve 2 tbsp of powder milk in 8 tbsp of water then use the required amount in the recipe (6 to 7 Tbsp) and drink the rest!
  3. If you plan to store your chin chin, be sure to let it cool down completely before storing it in an airtight container. This will make the chin chin remain crisp and last longer.

CHIN CHIN

Sweet crunchy deep-fried snacks made with flour, sugar, milk, butter, and aromatic spice powder like nutmeg or cinnamon.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 egg large
  • 5 to 6 tbsp milk
  • 2 1/2 tbsp melted butter
  • vegetable oil for frying
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon or nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Mix the flour, baking powder, sugar, and nutmeg together in a bowl.
  2. Add the egg, melted butter and milk to the dry mixture and mix together until a dough is formed and knead the dough for a couple of minutes (about 2 to 4 minutes).
  3. Roll the dough out into a thin sheet of about a 1/4 to 1/6 inch thickness and use a pizza cutter or a knife to cut the dough into tiny pieces.
  4. Preheat some oil - enough to deep fry the chin chin and fry until golden brown.
  5. Allow to cool completely before serving. Enjoy!

Boli & Ekpa

Roasted plantains with peanut butter and crushed peanuts natively known as 'Boli ati Ekpa' can be really filling and satisfying, giving you a feeling of being fuller for longer.

Ingredients

  • Ripe Plantain
  • Roasted groundnuts

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven.
  2. Rinse and peel plantain. Make not too deep incision to enable it roast well and faster.
  3. Arrange in baking pan and place in oven.
  4. Flip and cover again. Be careful when flipping because it will be soft to touch. It will be very firm once it's done. Enjoy!
